# Contributing Feedback

Patch Notes for Society improves through specific critique. The public v0.1 drafts are not final policy submissions; they are entry points for review, correction, and better implementation thinking.

## Best Ways To Contribute

Use the public feedback forms:

https://github.com/sethusehitch/patch-notes-society/issues/new/choose

The most useful contributions are:

- factual corrections;
- missing sources or better data definitions;
- implementation risks from people with operator experience;
- rights, safety, legality, or equity risks;
- metrics likely to be gamed;
- state, county, city, agency, nonprofit, or practitioner examples;
- suggestions about which issue should become a submission-grade policy memo first.

## What To Include

For issue-specific critique:

- name the issue and section;
- quote or summarize the claim you are challenging;
- explain what is wrong, incomplete, overstated, or underspecified;
- add a better source, definition, distinction, or implementation constraint if you have one.

For source corrections:

- include a public source link when possible;
- name the year, jurisdiction, definition, and limitation if relevant;
- say which claim the source supports, narrows, or contradicts.

For implementation risks:

- describe the failure mode;
- name who would notice first;
- identify the incentive, bottleneck, legal barrier, funding problem, staffing problem, or metric problem;
- suggest a guardrail, pilot design, pause condition, or counter-metric if possible.

## What Not To Post Publicly

Do not post:

- private personal information;
- confidential documents;
- identifying victim, patient, student, client, or case details;
- threats or targeted harassment;
- operational instructions that could enable harm;
- legal, medical, or crisis advice for an individual situation.

If feedback depends on sensitive or private information, summarize the public lesson without exposing the private facts.

## How Feedback Is Used

Public issues are triaged with labels and the `v0.2 Review Cycle` milestone.

Strong feedback may lead to:

- a public draft correction;
- a source appendix update;
- a metric or implementation-risk note;
- a policy memo revision;
- selecting an issue for deeper submission-grade work.

The goal is to make public systems easier to inspect, criticize, test, fund, govern, and improve.
