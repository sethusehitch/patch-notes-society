What This Is

Patch Notes for Society looks at public problems as inherited systems: history, incentives, failure modes, tradeoffs, metrics, migration plans, and revision based on critique.

These are public v0.1 entry points. The policy track is separate: evidence matrices, reviewer packets, and submission-grade memos under The Better Systems Project.

The standard: do not pretend the first draft is right. Make the system easier to inspect, criticize, test, fund, govern, and improve.

Reviewers Wanted

I am looking for critique from people who know these systems from the inside: researchers, operators, practitioners, lawyers, advocates, public officials, funders, community leaders, and people directly affected by these systems.

Useful feedback is specific:

  • Where is the history wrong or incomplete?
  • Which incentive is missing?
  • Which reform would backfire?
  • What metric would be gamed?
  • Which paper should become a formal policy memo first?
