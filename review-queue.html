Review Rule

Agreement is welcome, but critique is more valuable. The best feedback names a specific claim, source, missing category, implementation risk, metric that could be gamed, or population the paper handles too broadly.

What Different Reviewers Can Do

Researchers

Find overclaims, stale sources, weak causal claims, missing counterevidence, bad metrics, and places where the paper should downgrade certainty.

Operators and practitioners

Name the staffing, procurement, workflow, legal authority, data, funding, training, safety, or implementation constraint that would break the plan first.

Advocates and affected people

Point out whose experience is flattened, who would be burdened, where language creates harm, and what protection or accountability is missing.

Policymakers and funders

Pressure-test jurisdiction, budget, eligibility, measurement, pilot size, public legitimacy, failure conditions, and what should happen before scale.

Submit The Most Useful Kind Of Feedback

  • Issue-specific critique: what is wrong, missing, or overstated?
  • Source correction: what source should be added, removed, corrected, or narrowed?
  • Implementation risk: what would fail, backfire, or be gamed?
  • General feedback: what should the project change about format, sequencing, or review process?
